Let Us Eat Cake

Julian Aldridge
5 min read
·
May 26, 2021

--

How Challengers Manage to Have Their Cake and Eat it Too. By Julian Aldridge, Founder, Enact Agency

Who doesn’t want cake? I thought so, not many people. Cake comes in an almost limitless variety of varieties, from heavy Christmas cakes my mother used to load with fruit, brandy and sugar, to delicate little cupcakes dispensed from vending machines. From heart attack-inducing Black Forest Gateaux to Chinese Mooncakes reserved for the annual Mid-Autumn Festival. Regardless of your preference, cake rocks!

The origins of cake go back many thousands of years, probably to Viking times. Both the Greeks and Romans had words for this slightly sweeter variant of bread, and cake became a popular part of the diet of nobility in the middle ages, albeit not necessarily a very healthy part.

Cake is Synonymous With Treats

However, it took the French Revolution to really cement the idea of cake being a special treat, when the phrase ‘let them eat cake’ was (probably inaccurately) attributed to Marie Antoinette, as a potent example of how out of touch the ruling class were when it came to the peasantry.

Today, cake is everywhere. At birthdays, holiday celebrations and bar mitzvahs. In America, alone, over 100 million cakes are consumed each year. Cake is so loved that we even have an expression that extolls its specialness:

‘You can’t have your cake and eat it too’

Like many conventions in life, Challengers reject this premise. Here are three firms who are saying, ‘yes, you can have your cake, and eat it too.’ Sometimes literally.

Skinny Cow. If the world loves cake, it arguably loves ice-cream even more. However, there’s a large part of society that craves the taste of a delicious frozen dairy treat — but dreads the attached waist-expanding caloric consequences.

Sure, low-calorie, low-sugar, low-fat, low-cholesterol food has been around since the 1960s. Not that it appears to have done much good, given that the average American woman is now over 170 lbs, an increase of over 20% over fifty years. However, add tasty to any of the above and the choices shrink like a popped balloon.
